Revised Manuscript


Authors are advised to prepare their manuscripts before submission, using the following guidelines:

Format
  • All files should be submitted as MS Word compatible documents.
  • Times New Roman font, 12 sized and double line spaced. Single columned layout and in A4 sized paper.
Article Length and numbering
  • Articles can contain maximum of 20 pages including references.
  • All lines should be numbered during the submission.
Article Title
  • A title of not more than 15 words should be provided.
Article Title Page
  • An Article Title Page should be submitted alongside each individual article using the template provided. This should include:
    1. Article Title
    2. Author Details (see below)
    3. Structured Abstract (see below)
    4. Keywords (see below)
    5. Content (see below)
Author Details
  • Full name of each author
  • Affiliation of each author, at time research was completed. If more than one author has contributed to the article,
    of who should be contacted for correspondence
  • Official E-mail address of the corresponding author
  • ORCID id of the corresponded author
Abstract
  • A single paragraphed abstract containing maximum of 200 words. The abstract should include purpose of the study,
    design/methodology/approach, findings, research limitations/implications (if applicable),
    and also could mention the originality/value of the work.
Keywords
  • Provide up to five keywords encapsulating the principal topics of the paper.
  • Whilst we will endeavor to use submitted keywords in the published version, all keywords are subject to approval by
    SLJoT’s editorial team and may be replaced by a matching term to ensure consistency, if necessary.
Contents
  • Arrange the manuscript based on the following structure:
    • Title Page (with the title, authors name with affiliations, abstract and keywords)
    • Introduction
    • Materials and MethodMaterials and Method
    • Results (with sub headings)
    • Discussion (without sub headings)
    • Acknowledgement (if any)
    • References (APA Style)
    • Author contributions (names with their respective contributions towards the manuscript)
    • Tables and Figures (arranged at the bottom of the manuscript in order on separate pages)
References
  • References to other publications must be in APA Citation Style and carefully checked for completeness, accuracy and consistency.
Reviewers
  • Author should provide at least three (3) potential reviewers who can review their manuscript
Copyrights
  • The Authors should fill the copyright transfer form while they submit the paper,
    which can be downloaded from the Sri Lankan Journal of Technology official web site: http://seu.ac.lk/sljot/download.php.

Manuscripts prepared based on the detailed guidelines should be emailed to the Editor in Chief ( sljot@seu.ac.lk).

CHECKLIST prior to initial submission:
You must ensure that you have the following files available. Acceptable file formats are listed above. Please ensure
that all files are named cautiously and explicitly so that their content is clear:
  • A file of your complete manuscript text including title page and abstract
  • Include your high-resolution figures, schemes, and table tables after reference section with figure legends.
    Please make sure that each figure is clearly labelled with its number
  • Any supplementary data MUST be saved as a separate file from the main manuscript file
  • We request our Authors to use "Plagiarism Checking software’s" to check plagiarism prior to submission as a
    preliminary step. We encourage the authors to limit the similarity index to be below 15% of their submissions
    and beyond that limit will be rejected.
  • The email addresses of all of your co-authors need to be send when the manuscripts is submitted [Please note that
    the journal reserves the right to contact the Senior Author of the manuscript if his/her contact details are not included].
  • Names, institutes and email addresses of at least four suggested referees. They should be scientists working independently
    (i.e. not a collaborator) in areas comparable to your own who have relevant proficiency, such as those included in your
    reference list.
  • Your letter to the editor (cover letter), either as a separate file or for cutting and pasting into email.

All submitted manuscripts will be undergoing initial screening process to see the originality of the work as well as relevance and contribution. SLJoT does not encourage any form of plagiarism and duplicate submissions. Therefore, we strongly recommend our authors to thorough check of the article content before submitting it to our journal for publication. We request our Authors to use "Plagiarism Checking software’s" to check plagiarism prior to submission as a preliminary step. We encourage the authors to limit the similarity index to be below 15% of their submissions and beyond that limit will be rejected. To save time for authors and peer-reviewers, only those papers that seem most likely to meet our editorial criteria will be sent for formal review. Those papers judged by the editors to be of insufficient general interest or otherwise inappropriate will be rejected promptly without peer review (although these decisions may be based on informal advice from specialists in the field). Manuscripts judged to be of potential interest to our readership will be forwarded to for formal review, typically to two reviewers, but sometimes more if special advice is needed (for example on statistics or a particular technique). The double-blind peer review will be conducted by the local or international editorial board members of the journal or other specialists of equal repute. Reviewers are required to reveal any potential conflicts of interests that may affect their ability to provide an unbiased review of an article. The editors then make a decision based on the reviewers' advice, from among several possibilities:

  • Accept , with or without editorial revisions.
  • Invite the authors to revise their manuscript to address specific concerns before a final decision is reached.
  • Reject, but indicate to the authors that further work might justify a resubmission.
  • Reject outright, typically on grounds of specialist interest, lack of novelty, insufficient conceptual advance or major technical and/or interpretational problems.
